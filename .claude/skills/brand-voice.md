# Ascent Brand Voice

## What Ascent Is

AI-native growth agency for Series A and B companies. Not a consultancy. Not a full-service marketing agency. Not a retainer shop that bills hours and ships decks.

Three offerings:
1. **Signal Audit** — 5-day diagnostic that ranks every channel by revenue impact
2. **Growth Sprint** — 8-week engagement with defined outcomes agreed before week one
3. **Retainer Partnership** — post-sprint, quarterly targets, no hours billed

Core differentiator: AI-native execution, not AI-augmented traditional playbooks. Most agencies add AI tools on top of the same process. Ascent starts with data and signal, builds strategy from what it finds, and uses AI to execute at a speed and precision a traditional team cannot match.

**Brand purpose:** Growth that compounds. Every decision should make the next decision easier and the next result larger.

**Value proposition:** AI-native growth for companies ready to move.

---

## Voice

Direct. Precise. Founder-to-founder, not vendor-to-prospect.

- Short sentences. A sentence doing two jobs should be two sentences.
- Specific claims only. Every claim needs a number or example. If there is none, cut the claim.
- Active voice always. "We map the channels" not "Channels are mapped."
- No hedging. Remove "almost," "very," "really," "truly" as intensifiers.
- No agency speak. No "unlock," "leverage," "robust," "seamless," "ecosystem," "journey."
- No vague promises. No outcomes without specifics.
- Sounds like someone who has done this, not someone trying to win a pitch.

---

## Em Dash Rules

Em dashes are the most overused punctuation in AI-generated copy. Use them sparingly.

- Maximum one `&mdash;` per section of body copy.
- Use only when a genuine pause or aside is needed. Not to replace a comma.
- Never for decoration or rhythm.
- Never between two independent clauses when a period works better.
- In JSX/HTML: always use `&mdash;` (never a raw `—` character, which breaks encoding).
- No spaces around `&mdash;` in source: `word&mdash;word`.

## Words and Phrases to Never Use

| Banned | Why |
|--------|-----|
| unlock | Agency cliché |
| leverage | Same |
| robust, seamless, innovative | Meaningless adjectives |
| ecosystem, journey, navigate | Vague metaphors |
| game-changing, transformative | Empty superlatives |
| surface (as a verb) | Say "find" or "reveal" |
| "at the end of the day" | Filler |
| "in short," "to be clear," "that said" | AI transition clichés |
| "actually," "truly," "really" as intensifiers | Implies the reader doubted you; cut |
| growth consultancy | Wrong category. Use "AI growth agency" |
| compounding growth | Use "growth that compounds" |

## Anti-AI Patterns

These patterns appear constantly in AI-generated copy. Every one should be caught and fixed.

### 1. "The X is the Y"
> ~~The sequence is the strategy.~~
> ~~The signal is the answer.~~

Sounds profound, means nothing. Use once maximum across the whole site, and only if it earns it.

### 2. Fragment drama
> ~~Short. Punchy. Like this.~~

Write sentences. One idea per sentence, but complete sentences.

### 3. Em dash as comma substitute
> ~~We audit first — then we build — then we measure.~~

Use a comma or rewrite. Em dashes are for asides, not rhythm.

### 4. "Not X. Not Y. We are Z."
> ~~Not a retainer shop. Not a strategy deck.~~

Legitimate once per page maximum. AI overuses it because it sounds confident without saying anything.

### 5. Repeated "We" sentence openers
> ~~We audit first. We define outcomes. We only take clients we can grow.~~

Vary the sentence structure. Lead with the action, outcome, or a specific fact.

### 6. Rhetorical questions as section headers
> ~~What makes us different?~~

Don't ask a question you immediately answer. Make the statement instead.

### 7. "X, not Y" more than once per page
> ~~Results, not reports. Signal, not noise. Data, not guesswork.~~

Powerful once. Twice is a pattern. Three times is a tic.

### 8. Excessive parallel lists
> ~~We find. We build. We measure. We scale.~~

A list of four one-word sentences is AI filler. Write prose.

### 9. Vague outcome language
> ~~"...and that changes everything."~~
> ~~"...because it matters."~~

Specify the outcome or delete the sentence.

### 10. "Actually" as a qualifier
> ~~We actually find what's driving revenue.~~
> ~~what's actually working.~~

If the claim is strong, it doesn't need the qualifier. Cut "actually" everywhere.

### 11. Section openers that summarise instead of claim
> ~~"Growth without the right foundation is just activity."~~

This is the AI throat-clear. Skip to the point.

### 12. Stacking adjectives before nouns
> ~~"focused, AI-native, sprint-based engagements"~~

One adjective maximum per noun in marketing copy.

## Copy Checklist

Before finalising any piece of copy:

- [ ] Is "AI-native" or "AI" present where it should be (homepage, about, metadata)?
- [ ] Is every claim backed by a number or a named outcome?
- [ ] More than one em dash per body section? Remove the extras.
- [ ] Three consecutive sentences starting with "We"? Rewrite.
- [ ] Any anti-AI pattern from the list above? Fix it.
- [ ] Does it sound like a founder or a vendor?
- [ ] "Growth consultancy" anywhere? Replace with "AI growth agency."
- [ ] "Actually" used as a qualifier? Cut it.
- [ ] Any passive voice? Rewrite active.
